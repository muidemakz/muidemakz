UI/UX Case Study: Designing a quiz feature in an Educational App (Class Sensei)

By - Excellent .O. Makanju 23-03-2019

Overview

It’s a widely known occurrence that students don’t like STEM subjects such as Maths, Sciences, and Technological Course. Most of these students go into college and have to shuttle through College barely because the foundation wasn’t laid properly and most of them eventually end up taking Social Science courses or Art because they find it easy to learn and scale over. This happens a lot with teenagers who are just starting to figure out what path they want to follow as they slowly advance towards College. This is what gave birth to my App “Class Sensei”. The idea was to create an Educational App that would help students study better and hence, Class Sensei was created. Class Sensei is an Educational App where Students between the ages of 13–19 can come to and thoroughly learn Mathematics with as much fun as possible.

THE PROBLEM STATEMENT

The task was to create an Educational App for Teens on a STEM Subject but after research, I realized the real problem I wanted to solve was: How might we make STEM courses learnable for teens?

TARGET AUDIENCE

My primary target audience was students who don’t like STEM subjects especially Mathematics. The level at which students drop out has become quite alarming and I saw it as an opportunity to improve the statistics. But it’s a broad audience. I needed to trim it down so I could make more impact in their lives. I was now done with empathy, so I administered questionnaires to my neighbors, my family and friends’ children to give me an idea of why they like mathematics and if not what they think could help them better learn it.

I collated the feedback from the questionnaire and got some helpful insights from them. My target audience and focus have now shifted to concentrate on teenagers who don’t like mathematics. And I discovered that a mobile app would be the perfect tool. I found out that most of them didn’t like there Maths teacher and they associated it to why they didn’t like Maths and so that gave me an Idea. To create a likable Mathematics Teacher.

DEVELOPING MY SOLUTION

After collating my Questionnaire results. I also spoke/interviewed some adults on their understanding of Mathematics as they were growing up and they spoke on some of their pressure points. With both the questionnaire results and interviews concluded. I now had enough information to come up with a solution. I now needed to isolate the various problems to enable me to come up with a specific solution. So I then drew an Empathy Map to help me make an informed decision. From the response, I came up with a PERSONA, Dojo Dummy, 15 years old, just finished JSS3 and preparing to resume SS1 where they concentrate on either Sciences, Social Science or Arts. He is very impatient. Also extroverted. He doesn’t like being idle. With all the research data I already had, I started to ideate my designs. The first thing I did was to create a user flow for the app. I also set up a mood board to help me streamline my design options and then I created a wireframe to give me an idea of what I wanted the design to look like.

HOW I VALIDATED MY SOLUTION

Once I was done with all the research and wireframes. I designed a working prototype. I sent a link to some of the neighbors, family and friends children. I asked them to tell me what they thought and their feedback helped me fine-tune the prototype and made it ready to test with the Target Audience. I gave a few random teenagers to check and they loved it. Especially the Quiz feature. They liked challenging The Sensei and surprisingly it boosted their confidence in their respective Maths class in School. Still waiting for their results at the end of their Summer Lessons as proof.
